Invasive Species to Roseville California

What is a Introduced Species?

A introduced species is a non native species that is introduced into a new envoirnment and ecosystem other than it's native or natural range. It is brought over by human transportantion either on purpose or on accident. (Definition: A nonnative species brought to a new environment by human activity, either deliberately or accidentally)

What is a Invasive Species

A non-native organism (like a plant, animal, or microbe) that spreads rapidly and causes ecological, economic, or social harm. Basicaly, a non native introduced species that takes over the local native ecosystem whitch harms the enviornment.

Native - Apache Paper Wasp (Polistes Apachus) vs Invasive - European Paper Wasp (Polistes Dominula)

Native - Apache Paper Wasp (Polistes Apachus)

Invasive - European Paper Wasp (Polistes Dominula)

Apache wasps (Polistes apachus) is the native species that encompases Roseville California. On the other hand European paper wasps (Polistes dominula) is the introduced invasive species in Roseville California. Apache wasps are larger, yellow antennae,brightly colored, builds umbrella type nests, and is less agressive than European paper wasp. European paper Wasps have a slender body, black and yellow bands (Resembles a yellowjacket), red/orange antennae, nests underground, more aggressive than Apache paper wasp, more diverse diet, found to prey on Monarch Butterflies.

Invasive - German Yellow Jackets (Vespula Germanica)

Western Yellow Jacket (Vespula Pensylvanica) is the native species to Roseville California. Western Yellow Jackets typicaly nests in the ground, and less aggressive than German Yellow Jacket. German Yellow Jacket typicaly nest above ground, more aggressive than Western Yellow Jacket, and stings more frequently.

Native - Western Grey Squirrel (Sciurus Griseus) vs Invasive - Fox Squirrel (Sciurus Niger)
vs Invasive - Eastern Gray Squirrel (Sciurus Carolinensis)

Native - Western Grey Squirrel(Sciurus Griseus)

Invasive - Fox Squirrel (Sciurus Niger)

Invasive - Eastern Gray Squirrel (Sciurus Carolinensis)

Native - Western Grey Squirrel (Sciurus Griseus) is larger than Eastern Gray; 1.1 to 1.4 pounds; 500-615 mm long, similar to Eastern Gray, but less tolerant of human development, and silver-gray on top, with a white belly. Invasive - Fox Squirrel (Sciurus Niger) is largest; 1.75 to 2.5 pounds; up to 26 inches long including tail, more solitary, bolder, and ground-oriented, and variable (gray, brown, black), often with a facial mask. Invasive - Eastern Gray Squirrel (Sciurus Carolinensis) is smaller; 1 to 1.5 pounds; 17.25 to 18.25 inches long,more social and agile climbers, and gray with a brownish or reddish underside.
