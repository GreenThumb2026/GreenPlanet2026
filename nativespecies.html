What is a native species?

A native species is an organism that naturally occurs in a specific geographic area, ecosystem, or habitat due to natural processes like evolution, without human intervention. These species have evolved and adapted to their environment over time and are integral to the local ecosystem.

Keystone and Endemic Species

    What is a keystone Species?

    A keystone species has a large impact within it's envoirnment. It's presence of that species maintans that ecosytem in check of balance. A keystone species is an organism that plays a critical role in an ecosystem, having a disproportionately large impact on its structure and health relative to its abundance. Without a keystone species, the ecosystem would change drastically.

What is a Endemic Species?

A organism native to specifically a exclusive region and area, found nowhere else on earth. An endemic species is an organism native to and found exclusively in a specific geographic area, and nowhere else in the world. These species often evolve in isolation and are adapted to unique environments, which can make them particularly vulnerable to extinction if their habitat is disturbed.
